The Arms Arsenal - Beginner Dumbbell Edition is designed specifically for teens looking to build strength in their biceps, triceps, and forearms using simple yet effective dumbbell exercises. This course focuses on controlled movements, ensuring proper form and injury prevention while gradually increasing endurance and muscle definition. It includes progressive workouts that enhance arm strength, stability, and coordination while keeping sessions short and engaging.

Double Bicep Curls (3 sets of 12-8 reps): Hold a dumbbell in each hand with palms facing forward. Curl the weights towards your shoulders while keeping elbows tucked. Lower slowly and repeat. Common Mistake: Swinging the dumbbells or using momentum instead of controlled movement.

Wrist X Hammer Curls (3 sets of 12-8 reps): Roll the dumbbells down and hold them at the edges of your fingers. Curl them back up and bend the dumbbell into the forearm, creating tension. Common Mistake: Moving the elbows forward instead of keeping them stationary.

Negative Curls: A negative curl is a bicep curl where you focus on lowering the weight slowly and under control. This exercise can help increase strength and reduce the risk of injury. Hold a barbell with an underhand grip, stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, and take three seconds to lower the barbell back to the starting position.

Foundational Strength

Focuses on basic movement patterns, helping beginners establish proper form and technique. Teaches slow, controlled repetitions for muscle activation. Exercises: Bicep Curls, Overhead Tricep Extensions, Hammer Curls.

Stability and Control

Introduces slight variations to improve coordination and stability. Includes unilateral movements to correct muscle imbalances. Exercises: Alternating Dumbbell Curls, Dumbbell Kickbacks, Zottman Curls.

Endurance and Volume

Slightly higher repetitions to build muscle endurance. Incorporates tempo changes for enhanced muscle activation. Exercises: Dumbbell Lateral Raises, Concentration Curls, Close-Grip Dumbbell Press.

Strength Progression

Introduces compound arm exercises to increase overall strength. Focuses on grip strength and power. Exercises: Arnold Press, Dumbbell Cross-Body Curls, Reverse-Grip Curls.

Full Arm Burnout

Combines previous movements for a complete arm workout. Includes a finisher to push endurance and fatigue the muscles effectively. Exercises: 21s (Bicep Curls), Skull Crushers, Farmers Carry.
